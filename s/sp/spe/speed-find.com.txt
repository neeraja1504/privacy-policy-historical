4. Cookies and Online Advertising:
We use cookies, pixel tags, web beacons and other such similar technologies to collect and store the information we automatically collect about your computer, device and Service usage. A cookie is a small text file that is stored on your computer and a pixel tag (also called a web beacon or clear GIF) is a tiny graphic with a unique identifier, embedded invisibly on a webpage (or an online ad or email). We use the term “cookies” in this policy to refer to cookies and all such similar technologies.
A cookie is a file which is downloaded and stored in the computer to allow us, among other things, to store and recover information about the user's browsing habits or that of his/her equipment's and, depending on the information contained and the way in which the equipment is used. Our website can only establish cookies in his/her computer if his/her search engine allows so.
There are two broad categories of cookies: (1) first party cookies, served directly by us to your computer or mobile device, and (2) third party cookies, which are served by a third party on our behalf. Cookies have a lot of jobs. For example, our cookies enable us to recognize you (e.g. as a registered user) when you visit our website and to store your preferences and settings. We use third party cookies for functionality, performance / analytics, advertising and social media purposes. Cookies from advertising companies, for example, help to ensure that the advertisements you see online are more relevant to you and your interests.
In accordance with current legislation, we inform you that our software and our website include an advertising system or component consisting of an advertising cookie which allows us to identify the web page which the user is visiting with the aim of showing him/her advertising or informative messages through our software which may interest him/her while s/he searches, receiving the best offers of the day on the internet in his internet browsing window. Our website can only establish cookies in his/her computer if his/her search engine allows so.
In addition to technical and personalisation cookies which are essential for the correct working of our website, we use cookies with the following aims:
Analysis Cookies: they are only used to quantify the number of users accessing this website, simplifying the measuring and statistical analysis which may allow us to improve the offer of products and services in the Web-Site.
Advertising Cookies: They allow us to manage the offer of our own advertising spaces and that of our collaborators, matching the content of the advert to the content of the service requested or to the use made of our web page. This is done in accordance with his/her Internet browsing habits, being able, therefore, to show him/her adverts related to his search profile.
Behavioural advertising cookies: We allow run as efficiently as possible and advertising space to third-party websites accessed by the user. These cookies store information on user behaviour obtained by monitoring your browsing habits, allowing to develop a specific profile to display ads based on the same.
The analysis cookies for third parties that we use in our website are:

Cookie AppNexus: advertising cookie that provides advertisements in your browser.

The installation of cookies is always done if and only if the user authorises in his/her search engine the installation, not only in his pages but also on those connected with or referenced recently through the use of links, without collecting at any given time personal or confidential user data or behavioural profiles, guaranteeing the anonymity of the user.
How To Control Or Delete Cookies:
Most browsers allow you to change your cookie settings. Users can usually set their browsers to block all third-party cookies (which are those set by third-party companies collecting information on websites operated by other companies), block all cookies (including first-party cookies), or block specific cookies. These settings will typically be found in the “options” or “preferences” menu of your browser. In order to understand these settings, the following links may be helpful, otherwise you should use the “Help” option in your browser for more details.
Cookie settings in Internet Explorer
Cookie settings in Firefox
Cookie settings in Chrome
Cookie settings in Safari web and iOS.
Please note that if you choose to block cookies, this may interfere with certain functionality on our website. Also, if you have disabled one or more cookies, we may still use information collected from cookies prior to your disabled preference being set; however, we will stop using the disabled cookie to collect any further information.
Third parties whose products or services are accessible or advertised through the Services, including social networking services, may also use cookies or similar tools, and we advise you to check their privacy policies for information about their cookies and other practices. We do not control the practices of such partners and their privacy policies govern their interactions with you. We encourage you to read their privacy policies.

These companies, as well as data analytics companies who service them, may use cookies, pixel tags, mobile device IDs and other similar technologies to collect data about you when you visit our site. Do Not Track. Do Not Track (“DNT”) is a privacy preference that users can set in certain web browsers. DNT is a way for users to inform websites and services that they do not want certain information about their webpage visits collected over time and across websites or online services. We are committed to providing you with meaningful choices about the information collected on our website for third party purposes, and that is why we provide the Network Advertising Initiative and the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out links above.
