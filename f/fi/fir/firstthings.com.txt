Terms & Conditions | First Things
First Things is committed to maintaining the privacy of personal information that you provide us when using our website. This privacy policy describes how we treat personal information received about you when you visit FirstThings.com.
Information We Collect
When you use certain features of this site, we may collect personal information from you that may include your name, address and e-mail address. Also, we automatically collect non-personal information from you, such as “Internet Protocol (IP) addresses” and “cookies”. An IP address is a numeric address assigned to your computer. It is not linked to any personally identifiable information, so your name remains anonymous. Cookies are small pieces of information that are stored by your web browser on your computerâ€™s hard drive. Cookies allow the website to store and reference specific types of information while you are visiting a site. Information collected is used on an aggregate basis only; for example, to tell us how visitors commonly use and navigate this site.

We may use personal information in the following ways:
To send e-mail acknowledgements of subscription order or correspondence conducted on the website. To send you e-mail renewal notices. To enhance existing features or develop new features, products, and services. To provide First Things advertisers and other third parties with aggregate information about our websiteâ€™s user base and usage patterns. To process subscriptions, to verify your credit card data, and to send you a receipt.
Security
Our website employ security measures to protect against the loss, misuse, and alteration of the information under our control. We use a secure server to protect the security of your online transactions. Your information is transmitted using Secure Sockets Layer (SSL), the industry standard for sending encrypted information. Furthermore, all of the customer data we collect is protected against unauthorized access.
Other
When you use FirstThings.com, you consent to our collection and use of the personal information you provide. First Things does not disclose personally identifying information about its users to others without their consent. Please note that if at any time First Things is required by law or a legal process to release individual or organizational information, we must cooperate fully.

Our privacy policy may change, so please check back from time to time.
