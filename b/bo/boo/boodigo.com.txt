Boodigo - Your Privacy
Boodigo Privacy Policy
This Privacy Policy was last modified on October 29, 2014.
Boodigo ("us", "we", or "our") operates http://www.boodigo.com (the "Site"). This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use and disclosure of Personal Information we receive from users of the Site.
Well, this is going to be a fairly short read. Are you ready?
Boodigo does not collect any information from you, so we have nothing to share. We are dedicated to protecting our users' information. When you use our search engine, we do not know who you are or record what you searched for. You are as anonymous as we can reasonably make you.
IP Addresses: at no time will your IP address be recorded, nor do we record or log the relationship between your IP address and search term or history. In fact, we have no search history at all, which means more privacy for you. Definition of IP Address - An Internet Protocol address (IP address) is a numerical label assigned to each device (e.g., computer, printer) participating in a computer network
Data Collection: As stated above we do not collect any personal information, this includes but is not limited to; IP addresses, operating systems and platforms, browsers, email, where youi are` coming from or going.
SSL: We require all connections to be SSL which means that the person sitting next to you in the library while your “on an adventure with Boodigo” won’t be able to spy on your traffic. Definition of SSL - Secure Sockets Layer (SSL), are cryptographic protocols which are designed to provide communication security over the Internet.
Cookies: Boodigo does not set any permanent identifying cookies.
Information Sharing: As Boodigo does not collect information, we have no information to share. We are here to protect your right to privacy.
Requests from Law Enforcement Agencies: Bad news, if we are subpoenaed, we are legally obligated to comply. Good news, as we have not collected any information, we have no information to share. Caveat: While we take all practicable steps to protect the privacy rights of our users, if ordered by a court of law or a lawfully empowered governmental agency to modify and/or bypass some or all of our normal privacy protection systems, we will have no choice but to comply and may be forced to do so without notice.
Questions & Feedback: We always love to hear from our users. Please feel free to email us your questions and feedback about your experience.
