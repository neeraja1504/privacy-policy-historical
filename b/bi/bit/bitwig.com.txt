Bitwig | Privacy Policy
This privacy policy states which of your personal data we collect, process and use. Therefore, we kindly ask you to carefully read the following statements.
§1 Controller
Bitwig GmbH, Schwedter Str.13, 10119 Berlin, Germany, (hereinafter: „We“) is the operator of the website https://www.bitwig.com and therefore the controller of the personal data of the users (hereinafter: “You”) of the website in terms of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the German Data Protection Act (“BDSG- neu”).
§2 Contact Data Protection Officer
Fredrik Astevall, Bitwig GmbH, Schwedter Str. 13, 10119 Berlin, contact@bitwig.com.
§3 General References and Personal Data
(1) We protect your privacy and your personal data. We collect, process and use your personal data in accordance with the content of these data protection regulations and the applicable German data protection laws, including but not limited to the German Data Protection Act (“BDSG- neu”).
(2) Personal data in the sense of this internet privacy means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person. This includes but is not limited to your name, your e-mail address and possibly your address and your telephone number as well as your credit card and bank details and your value added tax specifications, should you be a registered trader.
(3) Personal data also include information about your usage of our website. In this context, we collect your personal data as follows: information about your visits to our website, such as the extent of the data transfer, the location from which you retrieve data from our website as well as other connection data and sources that you retrieve. For more detailed information see § 7 and § 8.
§4 Legal Basis for the Processing of Your Data
(1) Some personal data may only be processed with your consent in accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 lit. a GDPR. You can withdraw this consent at any time for the future. "For the future" means that your withdrawal has no effect on the processing of data that we have made between the granting of your consent and your withdrawal. You do not have to justify your withdrawal. An informal message via e-mail to contact@bitwig.com is sufficient.
(2) We process personal data that we require to fulfill or conclude a contract with you or to carry out pre-contractual measures on the basis of Art. 6 para. 1 lit. b GDPR.
(3) Other personal data we process according to Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f. GDPR for the protection of our legitimate interests, e.g. for the technically error-free and optimized provision of our services.
(4) In addition, it may be necessary to process personal data in order to fulfill a legal obligation that we are subject to (Article 6 para. 1 lit. c GDPR) or to protect your vital interests (Article 6 para. 1 lit. d GDPR).
§5 Duration of Storage
We delete or block your data as soon as the respective purpose of the storage is omitted. However, it may happen that the European or national legislator requires a longer storage period. In these cases, the data is not deleted or locked until the corresponding retention period has expired.
§6 No Disclosure of Your Personal Data
We will not disclose your personal data to third parties, except if you have consented to the data transfer or if we are authorized or obliged to transfer data due to legal provisions and/or official or court directions. This may refer to cases including but not limited to the provision of information for the purposes of law enforcement, danger prevention or the enforcement of intellectual property rights.
§7 Processing of Server Log Files
(1) Whenever you access our website, we collect the following information about your computer:
- your computer’s IP-address
- product and version information about the browser used
- the request from your browser
- the time of that request
- the status of that request
- the quantity of the transferred data with respect to that request
- the URL of the website from which you accessed our website
- the URL of the websites you access from our website
(2) The legal basis for this is Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR. The operator of a website has a legitimate interest in the storage of this data because he needs it to detect and eliminate errors of the website, to determine the utilization of the website or to make improvements.
(3) The purpose of this type of data processing follows from our legitimate interest mentioned above: This data is processed to detect and eliminate website errors in order to determine the utilization of the website or to make improvements.
(4) The data will be deleted within 7 days.
§8 Cookies
(1) We use cookies on our website. These are small text files that are stored on your device and that store certain settings and data for sharing with our system through your browser.
A cookie usually contains the name of the domain from which the cookie data was sent, as well as information about the age of the cookie and an alphanumeric identifier. Cookies allow our systems to recognize the user's device and make any presets available immediately. As soon as a user accesses the website, a cookie is transmitted to the hard disk of the respective user's device.
We use cookies, that are technically necessary and that process data such as language and Log-In information. In addition, we use cookies, that are not technically necessary, and that allow the processing of information about e.g. the frequency of website views and the use of certain website functions.
The cookies we use will only store the information about your use of the website as explained above. This is not done by an assignment to you personally, but by assigning an identification number to the cookie ("Cookie ID"). A combination of the cookie ID with your name, your IP address or similar data that would allow a mapping of the cookie to you does not occur.
(2) The legal basis for the processing of personal data using cookies is Article 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR, as we have a legitimate interest in improving the operation of our website and using the data for direct marketing purposes.
(3) We use the data that we process using cookies that are technically necessary in order to optimize our website and simplify its use.
We use the data that we process using cookies that are not technically necessary to align our website with your individual interests.
(4) The duration of storage varies depending on whether the cookies are session cookies or temporary cookies. Session cookies are automatically deleted after you leave our website. Temporary cookies are stored on your device for a certain period. Cookies that we use for statistical purposes are automatically deleted after a defined period.
(5) As a user you have full control over the use of cookies and the data processed in this way. If you do not want to use cookies, you can set your browser so that the storage of cookies will not be accepted. Please note that in this case you may be able to use our website only partially or not at all.

§11 Setting up a User Account
(1) In order to make full use of our services, you must create a user account on our website. To do this, you must enter the following data in the input mask:
- e-mail
- first and last name
- username
- password
- country
We have no access to your password. Changing the password is only possible by means of a password reset process.
(2) Since we require the data to be able to fulfil our contract with you, the legal basis is Art. 6 para. 1 lit. b GDPR.
Furthermore, the processing of data from your user account serves our legitimate interest, so that the processing is also based on Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR.
(3) Setting up a user account and the data transmitted to us in this context are essential for providing you with our services in full. You need to set up a user account in order to activate the software and receive free upgrades and a notification as soon as the eligibility expires.
Your user account’s data also enable us to determine whether you are the holder of a valid license and whether you comply with its terms, in particular whether you activate the software on no more than three computers.
§12 Activation of the Software’s Final Version / Download an Update
(1) Each time the final version of the software is activated, Bitwig GmbH installs an encrypted file which refers to the respective terminal device’s serial number in order to link the final version to that particular device (challenge-response authentication). Furthermore, when you activate the Bitwig software or install updates, we collect information about:
- your device (operating system, local user name, hardware device ID)
- the software itself (version, date of activation, successful and unsuccessful updates)
- the serial number of your product
(2) We use this data exclusively to activate the software and to provide you with updates.
(3) Since this data is necessary for the performance of the contract regarding the use of our Software, the legal basis for the processing is Art. 6 para. 1 lit. b GDPR.
(4) The information will be stored for as long as you have an active account.
§13 Crash Report

(2) The legal basis for this is Art. 6 para. 1 lit. f GDPR. The stored data enables us to fix the issue and provide you with a functioning software.
(3) We use this data solely to fix the issue.
(4) The data will be stored for as long as we need to fix the software.
