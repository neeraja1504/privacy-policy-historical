4. Personal Data collected during registration / payment
What Personal Information is collected and why?
If You register with the Service You must provide Your name and email address and You will be asked to choose a password for Your account. We use Your Personal Data to maintain Your account for use of the Service. We are entitled to do so under GDPR because we have a contractual relationship with You. This information helps to ensure that Your account is protected and allows 192.com to display private information to You about Your account. Your email address is used as Your Service user name, but it also provides a means of contacting You about Your account.
If You pay to access premium services on the Service it will be necessary to provide Your address, credit / debit card details. This enables the card transaction to be processed including checks to prevent unauthorised use of Your credit card by fraudsters. In performing these checks Your Personal Data may be disclosed to a registered Credit Reference Agency which may keep a record of that information. You can rest assured that this is done only to confirm Your identity, that a credit check is not performed and that Your credit rating will be unaffected.
Personal Data collected from You during registration / payment will never be published on the Service. It will be retained by us for up to 6 years from your last interaction with the Service in case it is required by regulatory or other authorities.
What will 192.com do with Your email address?
When You register providing an email address 192.com will seek Your consent to send You email containing news, offers and promotions. If you do not wish to receive such email you may opt out at this stage. You can unsubscribe at any time by going to "Your Account", and selecting "change e-mail preferences", or You can write to us at 192.com Customer Services, Unit 8-10 Quayside Lodge, William Morris Way, London, SW6 2UZ; by email at feedback@192.com; or by phone on 08000 887192.
Whether or not You opt into receiving this this type of email, we will use your email address to send You messages crucial to the use of Your account - for example, the initial account activation email and password reminders.
All email that we send to You utilises performance tracking technology. This means that we can capture information about which emails are sent to You, which emails You have opened, which links in those emails have been clicked, and the dates and times on which those emails have been opened or links in them clicked.
We use this information to help us understand which emails are useful to our users.
5. Search Logs
What search logs are collected and why?
192.com keeps logs of all searches conducted by users of the Service (whether registered or not) including the IP (Internet Protocol) address from which a user session was conducted, the identity of the searcher (where we have it), the date and time of each search made and the results provided in response to a search. This information may be used: i) to assist in improving the nature and quality of the Service; ii) to advise individuals whose Personal Information appears in search results and who have subscribed to "See Who's Looking For You" that their Personal Information has been displayed to a user of the Service and, if the searching user's identity is known, this information will be passed to the individual whose Personal Information has been displayed; and iii) 192.com also reserves the right to pass on such logs to the Police in the event that it might assist with an investigation. Personal Data contained within search logs will be retained for up to 6 years in case requested by regulatory or other authorities.

7. Use of Cookies on the Service
What are Cookies?
A Cookie is a text file containing small amounts of information that will be downloaded to Your computer when You use the Service. Each time You return to the Service this information will be automatically sent back to the Service servers enabling it to identify the computer that is connected to the Service and to recall Your personal settings for use of the Service. Cookies do not contain Personal Information. Cookies do lots of jobs and are a commonplace tool used by all major websites. They enable things such as navigating between pages efficiently, remembering user preferences, and generally improving the user experience. They can also help to ensure that adverts You see online are more relevant to You and Your interests.
There is legal regulation around how cookies are used and the agreement required from users and 192.com is committed to meeting these requirements.
What types of cookie does 192.com use and obtaining Your agreement?
The three categories of cookie that 192.com uses are:
Category 1 - Strictly necessary cookies
These cookies are essential in order to enable You to move around the Service and use its features. Without these cookies, 192.com could not provide the Service to You. You may not use our Service if you do not wish these cookies to be placed on Your computer.
Category 2 - Internal cookies (sometimes known as "performance" and "monitoring" cookies)
These cookies collect information about how visitors to the Service use the site, for instance which pages are used most often, and allow 192.com to remember choices You make such as Your user name. The information collected using these cookies cannot track Your browsing activity on other web sites. By using the Service, You agree that these cookies can be placed on your computer.
Category 3 - Targeting cookies
These cookies are used to deliver adverts more relevant to You and Your interests. They may also limit the number of times You see a particular advertisement as well as measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They are usually placed by third party advertising networks with 192.com's permission. They remember that You have visited a particular web site and this information together with information on Your inferred interests (based on how you have used that web site) is shared with other organizations such as advertisers. However, advertisers will never you're your identity unless you click on an advert and share those details with them. You can choose not to have these cookies placed on Your computer by going to:
www.youronlinechoices.com/uk/your-ad-choices
NAI: optout.networkadvertising.org
DAA: optout.aboutads.info
Unless You opt-out You agree that our partners may place these cookies on Your computer.
