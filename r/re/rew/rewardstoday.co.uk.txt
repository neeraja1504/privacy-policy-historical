Who is www.rewardstoday.co.uk
This Privacy Policy describes how Intela Limited ("Intela") treats the personal data collected from you on www.rewardstoday.co.uk ("Website"). The Website is operated and owned by Intela, a company registered in England and Wales (company number 06661813) with its registered office at Princess House, Suite 305, 50Eastcastle Street, London, United Kingdom W1W 8EA. We are registered with the Information Commisioner"s Office for the protection of personal data under number Z2142261. For the purposes of the Data Protection Act 1998 (the "Act"), the data controller is Intela Limited. Intela processes your personal data in accordance with this Privacy Policy and is governed by English law concerning the protection of personal data.
Intela deals with so-called opt-in marketing. By participating in activities via the Website you can win prizes. The rules for participation and prize draw can be found in our conditions of participation.
In this privacy policy a reference to "prize draws", "draws", "competitions" or "contests" will include a reference to any promotion where a user may or does qualify for a prize, gift, reward or other promotional item. A reference to a "prize" or "reward" will include a reference to any gift, reward or other promotional item earned. A reference to a "winner" will include a reference to a participant who has earned or qualified for a gift, reward or other promotional item.
What personal data do we collect?
Information provided by you through the Website
When you participate in activities on the Website, you are asked to provide information about yourself. This includes your name, address, date of birth, e-mail address and telephone number. We may also ask further questions regarding your marital status, education and for example about the price you want to receive, your interests, preferences and the receipt of products or information. Intela can also use your telephone number to send you a text message. These messages are for the verification of an order made.
Information provided by you via surveys
Intela or a client of Intela can contact you through telephone, email or Internet for a survey. When you participate in such a survey, Intela or the client which sends the survey shall process the personal data you provided in the survey. In such surveys you are asked to provide personal data such as your name, email address, address, postal code, telephone numbers, date of birth, gender, salary, education, and marital status, occupation, hobbies, interests, and (online) surfing habits.
Information provided by you to third parties
Intela can obtain information about you through third parties or "list rental". Intela acquires for example the following personal data through third parties: name, email address, address, postal code, telephone numbers, date of birth, gender, salary, education and marital status, occupation, industry of employment, hobbies, interests and online habits. Intela ensures that you have agreed that your data has been transferred to third parties. If you do not want your data to be processed by Intela any longer, you can fill out the unsubscribe form here. .
Information collected through cookies and third party pixels
Besides the data which is collected by Intela via the Website, via surveys and via third parties, Intela uses cookies and so-called third-party pixels to collect data about you.
A cookie is a small text file placed on your hard disk by the server of the Website. This allows the server to save your preferences and other data including passwords. Furthermore, Intela uses cookies to track the visits to the Website and to track and aggregate statistical data regarding site visits to gather information, for example for reporting purposes for its internal organization or for third parties. You can decide whether to accept or refuse cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies. However, if you use your browser settings to block all cookies (including essential cookies) you may not be able to access all or parts of the Website.
Intela uses the following cookies:
1. Strictly necessary cookies. These are cookies that are required for the operation of the Website. They include, for example, cookies that enable you to log into secure areas of our Website, use a shopping cart or make use of other services.
2. Analytical/performance cookies. They allow Intela to recognizes and count the number of visitors and to see how visitors move around the Website when they are using it. This helps Intela to improve the way the Website works, for example, by ensuring that users are finding what they are looking for easily.
3. Functionality cookies. These are used to recognise you when you return to the Website. This enables Intela to personalize content for you and remember your preferences (for example, your choice of language or region).
4. Targeting cookies. These cookies record your visit to the Website, the pages you have visited and the links you have followed. Intela will use this information to make the Website and the advertising displayed on it more relevant to your interests. Intela may also share this information with third parties for this purpose.

In addition to cookies the Website contains so-called "third party pixels", which are small .gif files consisting of one pixel. Intela's website contains pixels of third parties to collect data regarding the impact of their advertising campaigns. The pixels make it possible to see which sites you visited to analyze your online behavior and habits. Through the pixels we provide the following data to third parties: your IP address, the date of your visit and the time of your visit.
Other information
If you contact Intela, Intela can keep a record of that correspondence. Intela can also collect details of your visit to the Website, including but not limited to traffic data, location data, weblogs and other communication data including information about your computer, your IP address, operating system and browser type.
For what purposes do we use your personal data?
Intela uses the data you provide or which is otherwise obtained for the following purposes:
Awarding prizes and communication with winners about their prizes, including carrying out promotional activities regarding winners and their prizes;
Making offers on behalf of Intela or on behalf of its clients and partners. Click on the following link for an overview of clients and partners with whom Intela works. Intela can contact you on behalf of Intela or on behalf of clients and partners via e-mail, telephone or regular mail.
Providing your data to clients and partners so that they can approach you with targeted surveys, offers and information. Clients and partners of Intela who received your information can contact you via e-mail, telephone and regular mail;
Creating interest profiles for marketing purposes. Intela composes an interest profile based on your personal data. For this purpose a combination is made of data you have provided through surveys and any information obtained from third parties or data from public sources.
Who does Intela provide with your data?
Once you have given permission for the provision of your data to clients and partners of Intela, Intela sends your personal data to the concerned clients and partners.
Intela may hire other companies to manage its website or to provide you with products and services.These service providers (such as website and database hosting companies, directory providers, analytics companies, distribution companies, fulfillment companies and other similar service providers) may therefore have access to your information, but only to the extent necessary for the services provided to Intela.
Also, Intela can give third parties access to your personal data to comply with the law, to lawful requests or for conducting legal proceedings.
Furthermore, Intela can use or disclose your personal data if we believe that it is necessary to share that information in order to investigate, to prevent, or to be able to take action regarding illegal activities, suspected fraud, and situations involving potential threats to the physical safety of individuals.
Finally, Intela can transfer your personal data in the event of an acquisition, merger, sale or other change in its operational status and Intela can disclose your personal data to any member of its group, which means its subsidiaries, its ultimate holding company and such holding company"s subsidiaries, as defined in section 1159 of the UK Companies Act 2006.
How do we deal with personal data of minors?
Intela does not knowingly collect data from persons under the age of eighteen. As soon as it becomes known to Intela that personal data of a person under the age of eighteen are included in its database, it will remove this data. 6.How do we protect your data?
When you provide personal data via the Website, or when third parties provide personal data to Intela, Intela ensures the adequate security of your personal data. All data is stored in databases protected by among other things firewalls, to protect data from loss, abuse or accidental changes.
The personal data that Intela collects from you can be transferred to, and stored at, a destination outside the European Economic Area ("EEA"). It can also be processed by staff operating outside the EEA who work for Intela or for one of its suppliers. Such staff maybe engaged in, among other things, the fulfilment of your order, the processing of your payment details and the provision of support services. By submitting your personal data, you agree to this transfer, storing or processing. Intela will take all steps reasonably necessary to ensure that your data is treated securely and in accordance with this Privacy Policy.
Unfortunately, the transmission of information via the internet is not completely secure. Although Intela will take reasonable steps to protect your personal data, Intela cannot guarantee the security of your data transmitted to the Website; any transmission is at your own risk. Once Intela has received your information, Intela will use strict procedures and security features to try to prevent unauthorised access.
What is the relationship between Intela and third party websites?
The website of Intela and e-mail messages sent by Intela contain links to other websites. Intela accepts no responsibility for the content of these websites.
How can I unsubscribe from Intela and its partners and clients?
Unsubscriptions from Intela
You have the right to ask Intela not to process your personal data. If you no longer wish to be contacted by e-mail by Intela, please fill out the unsubscribe form here. Also for e-mails sent by Intela on behalf of its partners, you can unsubscribe through by filling out the unsubscribe form here.
Unsubscriptions from clients and partners of Intela
Once you have agreed to provide your data to clients and partners of Intela, these third parties can approach you with offers and information. You have the right to ask these third parties not to process your personal data. To do so you will have to contact the third party directly.
How can I access my information?
The Act gives you the right to access information held about you. Your right of access can be exercised in accordance with the Act. Any access request may be subject to a fee of £10 to meet Intela's costs in providing you with details of the information Intela holds about you.
Changes to Intela's Privacy Policy
Any changes Intela makes to this Privacy Policy in the future will be posted on this page and, where appropriate, notified to you by e-mail.
How can I get into contact?
If you want to change your personal data or have other questions about how Intela processes your personal data, please contact us at info@rewardstoday.co.uk .
