Definitions
Questionity platform is what we name the Web application which offers services to the social network.
Q!net is what we name a custimization of Questionity around a topic, both the interface as for the contents shown in the Q!net. A Q!net is part of the Questionity platform, therefore a user can access any Q!net of Questionity.
The Q!net has its own URL.
Signing up to Questionity can be done from Questionity or from any of its Q!nets. In any case, the user signs in to the Questionity platform and he/she will be able to access Questionity and its Q!nets with the same credentials.
The content published from a Q!net is part of the Questionity platform y can be shown in Questionity and/or in other Q!nets, besides of the Q!net from which it was originally published.
These General terms of use are valid for Questionity an its Q!nets. Unless specifically indicated, hereafter, the term Questionity refers to Questionity and its Q!nets.
Profile how we name the space of a user in Questionity where he/she becomes known, looks at and organizes his/her posts , receives opinions and answers from the users he/she is following, looks at his/her mentions and organizes his/her users groups, etc.
The users Profile is the same for Questionity and its Q!nets.
“User” is how we name any person which enters and browses through the social network of Questionity without having passed the Sign in porcess at Questionity.
Users can find and visualize public information of the platform (partial access), but won’t be able to publish nor to perform actions within the social network (full access).
Likewise, he/she neither will have a profile on Questionity’s platform.
A “Registered User” is any person which starts to go through the registering (Signin) process and therefore can iniciate his/her session in Questionity.
The registering process is completed, when this registration is confirmed with an email, to which the user responds after having signed in to Questionity.
The terms set out in these conditions apply to registered users.
Unless explicitly stated, hereinafter the term user refers to a registered user.

Once you become a registered user in Questionity you will be able to send invitations to your friends so they can also register with Questionity.
For this purpose, you will provide your friend’s email through the invitation system provided by Questionity. In any case, your friend will have to decide whether or not to register with Questionity, accepting the invitation and finishing the registration process.
Questionity will not store the email of your friend to whom you send the invitation, hence you will be responsible for whether your friend wants to receive such invitation or not, so you need to make a responsible use of Questionity invitation process.

Navigation
In order to improve navigation (for example, suggesting posts or users to follow, Q!nets of the platform to join where you can publish your posts), throughout our platform we use cookies and store IP’s. A cookie is a file we deposit on your equipment, and its only purpose is to simplify your navigation through our platform(this cookie can’t contain a virus nor can it be executed, because it is not an active file). This cookie can only be read by Questionity and yourself, and you can delete it if you wish, accessing the options of your web-browser.If you do not want cookies to be saved, you can configure your browser for this purpose.
In general, most browsers allow the management of cookies as follows:
1. Not accepting cookies.
2. Asking individually whether to accept or not each cookie.
3. Accepting cookies always by default.
4. Specifying the domains of the cookies to be accepted.
Questionity uses Google Analytics, an analytics web service provided by Google, Inc., a Delaware company with headquarters at 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View (California), CA 94043, Estados Unidos (“Google”). Google Analytics uses “cookies” which are text files placed on your computer, to help the website analyze how users use the web site. The information generated by the cookie about your use of the website (including your IP address) is transmitted to and stored by Google on the United States servers. Google will use this information on our behalf in order to track your use of the website, compiling reports on website activity for website operators and providing other services relating to website activity and Internet use. Google may also transfer this information to third parties where required to do so by law or where such third parties process the information on Google’s behalf. Google will not associate your IP address with any other data held by Google. You may refuse treatment of data or information by rejecting the use of cookies and selecting the appropriate settings on your browser. However, you should know that, by doing so, you may not be able to use the full functionality of this website. By using this website, you consent the treatment of your information in the manner and for the purposes described above.
