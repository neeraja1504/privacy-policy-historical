1. Summary of Services
Crowdtilt Open is a professional and customizable reward-based crowdfunding and e-commerce software and communication service (the "Service"). The Service is hosted by Crowdtilt Open, but may be accessed at one of our customer's domain addresses.
Crowdtilt Open is a website and service of Crowdtilt, Inc. or its subsidiaries ("Crowdtilt," "Crowdtilt Open,"" "we," "ours" or "us.") Please read this Privacy Policy (the "Privacy Policy") and make sure you understand it.
This Privacy Policy describes how Crowdtilt Open collects, uses, stores, shares and protects your information in connection with the Service, whether accessible via Crowdtilt Open's domain name or one of our customer's own domain names, co-branded websites (including sub-domains, international versions, and application versions) or our other services, applications, widgets and content provided through or in connection with the Service (collectively, the "Services").

This Privacy Policy applies when you access, visit or use the Service or a website using the Service and is part of, and is governed by, the terms and conditions set forth in the Crowdtilt Open Terms of Use and Checkout Terms.
By creating a Crowdtilt Open account, or by otherwise accessing, visiting, using, or transacting through the Service, you expressly consent to our collection, use, disclosure and retention of your information as described in this Privacy Policy and in the Crowdtilt Open's Terms of Use and Checkout Terms, which ever is applicable.
2.2. Modifications
We will notify you of changes to this Privacy Policy by posting the amended Privacy Policy on Open.Crowdtilt.com at least 30 calendar days before the effective date of the changes. If you have provided us with your email address we will also notify you of material changes to this Privacy Policy by sending an email at least 30 calendar days before the effective date of the changes to the email address you most recently provided to us. We encourage you to keep the email address you provide to us current, and to promptly notify us of any changes to your email address, so that you may receive any notices we send to you regarding material changes to this Privacy Policy. Otherwise, the new Privacy Policy will take effect after 30 calendar days.
3. Information Collection Practices
Information You Provide to Us. We may collect information that you provide to us in connection with your use of the Services when you (i) register as a user of the Service; (ii) make changes to your user profile information, (iii) send e-mail messages, forms, or other information to us through the Service, or (iv) use the Services, such as make a transaction through the Services. If you register for a Crowdtilt Open account directly through the Service, you will be required to provide your email address and create a password. You may also have to provide additional information in connection with your Crowdtilt Open account, including your full name, birthday, your city, state and country of residence, your zip code, and social security number. To collect money being held for you by the Service, you will need to provide payout information, including you bank account information, and compliance and fraud prevention related information.
Information Automatically Collected Through the Service. Crowdtilt Open, along with our third party service partners, vendors and business partners may automatically collect information about usage of your Crowdtilt Open site and those who visit and transact through it. The information automatically collected about those accessing or using your Crowdtilt Open site may include, but is not limited to, device ID, device type, browser type and version, geo-location information, computer and connection information, statistics on page views, traffic to and from the Service, ad data, IP address and standard web log information.
Some or our third party service partners, vendors and business partners allow your users to opt-out from the automatic collection of your information.
To opt-out from having Google Analytics collect and use your information, please visit https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.
To opt-out from having ClickTale collect and use your information, please visit http://www.clicktale.net/disable.html.
Cookies and Web Beacons. Crowdtilt Open, along with our third party service partners, vendors and business partners may use cookies, locally stored objects and web beacons to automatically collect information about your use of the Services. Cookies are small bits of information that are transferred to and stored in separate files within your computer's browser. The Services uses both "persistent cookies" which remain on your computer after you have closed your browser as well as "session cookies" which exist only during a visitor's online session and disappear from your computer when you close your browser. Locally stored objects or "flash cookies" are data files that can be created on your computer by the websites you visit and are a way for websites to store information for later use. Locally stored objects are different than cookies because they are stored in different parts of your computer than cookies. Web beacons are small strings of code that provide a method for delivering a graphic image on a web page or in an email message for the purpose of transferring data. You can set your browser to reject cookies or to notify you when you are sent a cookie. You can also disable the storage of flash cookies. For additional information about managing and disabling flash cookies, please visit http://helpx.adobe.com/flash-player/kb/disable-local-shared-objects-flash.html. However, if you reject cookies or disable flash cookies you may not be able to use all portions or all functionality of the Services.

12. California Privacy Rights

You may make one request each year by emailing us at Privacy@Crowdtilt.com or writing us at Crowdtilt, Attn: Legal Department, 370 Townsend Street, San Francisco, CA 94107.
