6 COOKIES AND SOCIAL MEDIA
In order to improve the Site and the Services provided thereon and to provide more convenient, relevant experiences to our customers, we and our agents may use “cookies”, “web beacons” and similar devices to track and analyse your activities.
A cookie functions as your identification card and enables TME to record your passwords and preferences. A cookie cannot be executed as code or deliver viruses.
A web beacon is a small transparent .gif image that is embedded in an HTML page or email used to track when the page or email has been viewed.
A similar tool may be used that may send data to a server when a set of user-initiated events occur. This tool is similar to a cookie. It tracks your visit and the data can only be read by the server that receives the data. It is different than a cookie in that it is not browser-based, may not function as an identification card and does not store any data on your computer.
There exist cookies that are technically necessary or unnecessary:
Necessary Cookies
Some cookies fulfil necessary tasks and websites cannot function properly without them. If necessary cookies are used, they cannot be removed. Necessary cookies may already be implemented when entering the website. That means their use does not necessitate a prior consent of the users.
Unnecessary Cookies
Cookies which not only serve the functionality of the website but also collect data for other purposes are considered unnecessary cookies. Unnecessary cookies allow us to track your navigation of our Site and to make the connected services visible. We may transfer your IP address to third parties in order to receive statistical data about the use of our Site. Such anonymized data helps us when designing the Site and the structure of our servers. Unnecessary cookies can be e.g. Social Plugins, cookies for analysis, marketing or advertising.
Unnecessary cookies may only be processed with the prior consent of the user.

**III. The Facebook page of TME
By clicking on the Facebook link you will be forwarded to our TME Facebook page (“Facebook Fan Page”). We determine the purposes and goals of the data processing taking place on this Site jointly with Facebook Ireland Limited and are therefore jointly responsible in line with article 26 GDPR.
A. How TME processes your data
1.   Facebook Insights
Description and extent of data processing:
Facebook Ireland Limited (“Facebook”) provides us with “Facebook Insights” which is implemented on all Facebook fan pages. Based on parameters and time frames Facebook provides us with statistical information. This statistical data contains various information on the following user categories:
Our fans: User, wo “liked” our Facebook Fan Page;
Reached user: User who were shown our posts within the last 28 days;
Involved user: User who “liked” our posts within the last 28 days, who commented or shared them or came into contact with our Facebook Fan Page.
From this data we cannot identify certain persons. This statistical data contains various information, for example on:
The activities and interactions (e.g. clicks, views), “Likes” and reports about our Site, our advertisements, our posts and our events;
The number and time of activity of our subscribers and visitors as well as statistical information about age, gender, country and city of the user and the language which the user speaks. If employed by a user, the name of the user;
Video statistics;
Received and sent messages.
Purpose of the data processing:
By using statistical data we gain statistical knowledge about how our Facebook Fan Page is used. This enables us to track user behavior in order to analyse it for business purposes like establishing of trade marks, increase of a connection to the community and communication.
Legal basis of the data processing:
Before you enter our Facebook Fan Page we need your consent in order to process your above mentioned data for the above mentioned purposes.
2. Facebook notifications
Description and extent of the data processing:
If you interact with our page or one of our posts, advertisement activities or events (e.g. by sharing or commenting), or if you subscribe to our page or follow us we will receive a notification from Facebook containing your name, your profile picture and your interaction.
This data can be accessed by responsible persons within the Toshiba Memory Group as well as third parties which support us as concerns Public Relations and Social Media Management. These service providers are contractually bound to observe our data privacy principles and confidentiality. Service provider who process personal data for us signed a special agreement to ensure that the data processing only takes place in line with our instructions.
We are not able to delete Facebook notifications, your posts or other interactions.
Purpose and legal basis of the data processing:
We use the data provided in order to track how you interact with our Facebook page and to react to your interactions (e.g. comments, “likes”, sharing). The data processing is based on our legitimate interest to fulfil this purpose (art. 6 para 1 no. f GDPR).
3. Contact via the Facebook Fan Page
Description and extent of the data processing:
If you contact us via Facebook we will process the information provided by you, e.g. your user name, your profile picture, your request and your contact information. You may contact us without providing your real name or other contact information.
This data can be accessed by responsible persons within the Toshiba Memory Group as well as third parties which support us as concerns Public Relations and Social Media Management. These service providers are contractually bound to observe our data privacy principles and confidentiality. Service provider who process personal data for us signed a special agreement to ensure that the data processing only takes place in line with our instructions.
We store your data for as long as it is necessary to fulfil the purpose described below but we cannot delete your posts, comments or other interactions on Facebook.
Purpose and legal basis of the data processing:
We process your data in order to answer your request. The data processing is based on our legitimate interest to fulfil this purpose (art. 6 para 1 no. f GDPR).
4. Raffles
Description and extent of the data processing:
If you participate in raffles on our Facebook Fan Page we will process the personal data provided by you like your user name, your profile picture, your first name and your contact information. We will use the user name of the winner in a comment and ask the user to provide us their postal address by e-mail.
This data can be accessed by responsible persons within the Toshiba Memory Group as well as third parties which support us as concerns Public Relations and Social Media Management. These service providers are contractually bound to observe our data privacy principles and confidentiality. Service provider who process personal data for us signed a special agreement to ensure that the data processing only takes place in line with our instructions.
We store your data for as long as it is necessary for the performance of the raffles. We are not able to delete your posts, comments or other interactions on Facebook.
Purpose and legal basis of the data processing:
We process your data exclusively in order to conduct the raffles. The legal basis for the data processing is the fulfilment of the contract (art. 6 para 1 no. b GDPR).
B. How Facebook processes your data
If you visit our Facebook page, Facebook collects and processes various personal data which is gathered by using cookies or other tracking technology and was provided by you or third persons. This can be for example personal data in order to compile statistics for Facebook Insights, to provide options to measure or analyse for advertisers, for market research and to personalize characteristics and contents. Facebook forwards this data to other companies within the Facebook Group and to third parties and transfers it to third countries outside of the European Union, for example the United States.
