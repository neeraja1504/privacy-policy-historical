1. Definitions
a. Automatically Collected Information
When You access the Site, we may send one or more cookies to Your computer.  A cookie is a small text file containing a string of alphanumeric characters that a website stores on Your computer that Your browser provides to a website each time You access the website.  Use of cookies is very common on the Internet. We use these cookies to identify prior Users and track usage of the Site. You can remove these cookies and modify Your browser’s settings to accept all cookies, reject all cookies, or notify You when a cookie is sent.  Please review Your web browser’s “Help” file to learn the proper way to modify Your cookie settings.
In addition, like many websites, when You access the Site, we may automatically record certain information from Your system by using different types of other common Internet technology.  This automatically collected information may include Your Internet Protocol address (or “IP Address”), a unique device or User ID, version of software installed, system type, the content and pages that You access on the Site, and the dates and times that You visit Site.
b. Personally Identifiable Information
Consumer Injury Advocates only collects personally identifiable information that is voluntarily provided to us by Users.
2. The Way We Use This Information
a. Automatically Collected Information
Consumer Injury Advocates uses cookies and other automatically collected information to identify prior Users, to track usage of the Site, and for analytical purposes to help us improve the Site and our business in general. We will provide this information to Affiliates and/or third parties to assist us in providing these functions.
b. Personally Identifiable Information
The personally identifiable information that Users voluntarily provide to Consumer Injury Advocates may be used by Consumer Injury Advocates and our Affiliates, including contracted third-parties for administrative purposes; for authentication purposes; to properly operate, maintain, and provide You with the features and functionality of the Site; to present You with promotions, advertisement, and opportunities which may be of interest to You via direct mail, email, phone, text, or sms; to enable our Affiliates and to assist us in operating, maintaining, and providing our Services and products to You via direct mail, email, phone, text, or sms; to enable us to carry out our obligations arising from any contracts/subscriptions entered into with You; to notify You about changes to the Privacy Policy or the Terms of Use, Site, or Service (if we deem it appropriate); to investigate and help prevent potentially unlawful activity or activities that threaten the integrity of the Site or Consumer Injury Advocates Technology; to investigate allegations of fraud or violations of the Terms; to enforce or apply our rights under the Terms or Service Agreement; to protect the rights, property, or safety of any of our Clients or others; to improve the quality and design of the Site and to create new features, promotions, functionality, and services by storing, tracking, and analyzing your preferences and trends; and/for marketing and business purposes. Consumer Injury Advocates RESERVES THE RIGHT TO, AND WILL, RENT, SELL, SHARE OR TRANSFER YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION TO THIRD PARTIES FOR ANY LEGALLY PERMISSIBLE PURPOSE IN ITS SOLE DISCRETION.
3. Security of Information
Consumer Injury Advocates maintains our systems in accordance with commercially reasonable industry standards to preserve the integrity and security of Your personally identifiable information under our control.  We cannot, however, ensure or warrant the security of any information You Transmit to us, and You do so at Your own risk.  Once we receive Your transmission of information, we make commercially reasonable efforts to ensure the security of our systems.  However, please note that this is not a guarantee that such information may not be accessed, disclosed, altered, or destroyed by breach of any of our physical, technical, or managerial safeguards.   “Perfect security" does not exist on the Internet, and You use the Site at Your own risk.
4. Children
Consumer Injury Advocates DOES NOT SELL PRODUCTS OR SERVICES FOR PURCHASE BY CHILDREN.  IF YOU ARE UNDER 18 YEARS OF AGE, THEN PLEASE DO NOT USE OR ACCESS THE SITE AT ANY TIME OR IN ANY MANNER.
PROTECTING THE PRIVACY OF YOUNG CHILDREN IS ESPECIALLY IMPORTANT.  FOR THAT REASON, Consumer Injury Advocates DOES NOT KNOWINGLY COLLECT OR MAINTAIN PERSONALLY IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION FROM PERSONS UNDER 13 YEARS-OF-AGE. IF Consumer Injury Advocates LEARNS THAT PERSONALLY-IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION OF PERSONS LESS THAN 13-YEARS-OF-AGE HAS BEEN COLLECTED ON OR THROUGH THE SITE, THEN WE WILL TAKE THE APPROPRIATE STEPS TO DELETE THIS INFORMATION.
IF YOU ARE THE PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN OF A CHILD UNDER 13 WHO HAS PROVIDED US WITH PERSONALLY IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION, THEN PLEASE CLICK HERE TO HAVE THAT INFORMATION DELETED IMMEDIATELY.

6. Opting Out
Consumer Injury Advocates only collects information that You have voluntarily provided to us.   You may “opt-out” of having Your personally identifiable information used for certain purposes, when providing us with this information.  You may decline to provide personal information to us and/or refuse cookies in Your browser, although some of our features or services may not function properly as a result.
Should You no longer wish to receive newsletter and promotional communications, You may opt-out of receiving them by following the instructions included in each newsletter or communication or by emailing or calling us per the information in Section 11 of the Privacy Policy.   You may continue to receive email communications from us up to ten business days from the time we receive Your unsubscribe request. Please note that Consumer Injury Advocates can only control its own mailing list and policies.  Third party advertisers that maintain their own mailing lists may send communications that advertise our services, and You may need to contact these parties directly in order to stop receiving their communications.
